The DO's

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times, especially in congested areas.
  • Keep your passport and emergency numbers with you at all times.
  • Be discreet.
  • Use a humble, guarded approach in encountering strangers.
  • Map out safe walking routes in advance.
  • Learn your way back to lodging, in case you get separated.
  • Heed local guidance about crime, terrorist activity, or unrest.
  • Avoid public disturbances and demonstrations.
  • Keep your purse or wallet in front of you to avoid its theft.
  • Hold firmly briefcases, computer cases, or other bags, but be prepared to let them go to avoid injury.
  • Walk purposefully and confidently, as if you know what you are doing and where you are going (even if you don’t!). Travel in groups when possible.
  • Stand with other people or groups on platforms and in trains.
  • When riding the subway, place yourself in a position where it would be difficult for someone to easily walk past and escape such as near the subway car doors.

The DON'Ts

  • Do not ride in an empty train car.
  • Do not sleep on public transportation.
  • Don’t go anywhere alone, if you have other options.
  • Don’t travel at night (walking, driving, or riding), if possible.
  • Don’t take shortcuts through alleys, deserted roads, or paths.
  • Don’t get distracted or engrossed in shopping or sightseeing.
  • Don’t complain, talk loudly, or boisterously, or otherwise attract undue attention to yourself.
