When to Report an Accident

  • You must report any accident involving serious personal injury (regardless of hospitalization), death, or property damage of over $5000 within 24 hours.
  • Your local motor vehicle manager must provide a complete and full report of the circumstances.
  • Any accident resulting in damage must be reported ! and investigated before repairs occur.

Report Motor Vehicle Accidents (MVAs) to:

  • You must report all domestic motor vehicle accidents involving CDC vehicles, regardless of the amount of damage, to the CDC fleet manager and Occupational Health and Safety Office point of contact.
  • International MVAs must be reported to the local fleet manager and your CDC Country Office point of contact.

What to Report

Include the following information when reporting an accident:
  • Accident report.
  • Police report.
  • Operator or investigator. Motor Vehicle Accident Report (SF-91).
  • Daily Motor Vehicle Use Record (OF 108).
  • Annual inspection and general maintenance records of the vehicle involved.
  • Copy of your driver’s license.
  • Records of your driver’s training course.
  • Medical clearance: Any drivers hired outside of the U.S. should have medical clearance to operate our vehicles. Many of these forms vary in different countries.
