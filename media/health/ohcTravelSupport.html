OHC Travel Support

Employees responding to outbreaks or participating in field investigations should contact the OHC prior to departure to ensure you have what you need to keep yourself safe and healthy.

Preparing to Travel

Many travel immunizations and medications need to be started before you leave. Schedule a pre-travel visit as soon as you have a final itinerary. (See Deployment Medical Clearance Here)

If you are an employee assigned outside of Atlanta and Ft. Collins, you can call the OHC for a telephonic pre-travel visit and referrals for any necessary CDC-covered services (at no cost to you).

During the pre-travel visit, you will:

  • Review medical history/status, prior travel experiences, etc.
  • Review destination-specific guidance and warnings.
  • Receive appropriate immunizations, prophylaxes, post-exposure guidance, and treatments.
  • Receive a helpful travel kit (if you visit a CDC Clinic).

Past Medical History and Personal Health

Your past medical history and current health can affect your fitness for international travel. Some staff members should not travel to areas of increased risk or limited medical resources to treat what is, in the United States, a manageable medical condition. If you have concerns about your fitness for travel to a particular destination, discuss them with the clinic staff or your travel medicine provider.

If you still have concerns after the pre-travel visit, discuss them with your personal health care provider.

Consider the following and discuss with health care advisors:

  • DO NOT travel until your health improves, if you have a significant acute, evolving, worsening, or undiagnosed condition or an inadequately controlled chronic disease.
  • DO NOT travel now, if you recently had surgery or have been told you need surgery or a diagnostic procedure.
  • Discuss the possibility of taking a supply of medication with you, if you are taking any prescription medications (including as-needed medications such as inhalers or creams).
