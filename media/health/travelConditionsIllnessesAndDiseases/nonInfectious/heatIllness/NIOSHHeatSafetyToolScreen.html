Heats Illnesses

The Heat Safety Tool is a useful resource for planning outdoor work activities based on how hot it feels throughout the day. Take precautions while working against outdoor heat with the OSHA-NIOSH Heat Safety Tool.

The app features:

  • Real-time visual heat index indicator and hourly forecasts based on your current location.
  • Precautionary recommendations specific to heat index-associated risk levels from OSHA and NIOSH.
  • Editable location, temperature, and humidity controls for calculation of variable conditions.
  • Information on the signs and symptoms of heat- related illnesses including: heat stroke, heat exhaustion, rhabdomyolysis, heat cramps, and heat rash.
  • First aid information for heat-related illnesses.

The heat index can be used as a screening tool, so that supervisors and workers can use heat indexes to more easily recognize when additional heat protective options should be used.

Work safely in the heat, download the Heat Safety Tool!

Using the OSHA-NIOSH Heat Safety Tool app

Heat Index

If your location services is enabled, the temperature and humidity data automatically downloads and the current heat index is displayed. Beneath the calculated heat index is the associated “Precautions” button for the risk level. Click on “Precautions” to go to the screen with risk level-specific recommendations.

Hourly Heat Index Forecast

If you are interested in planning your work activities for the entire shift around the heat index, the hourly feature allows you to scroll through and determine the hottest hours of the day along with the corresponding risk level and precautions.

Symptoms and First Aid

At the bottom of your app screen you will always have easy access to heat-related illness symptoms and first aid.

More

The “More Tips” provides information about being prepared for emergencies, training, acclimatization, hydration, monitoring workers for heat-related illness, and breaks. There is also a list of risk factors associated with heat-related illnesses.
