Dengue Fever

Dengue fever is a viral disease transmitted by Aedes aegypti mosquitoes, which typically bite from dawn to dusk (during the day). An estimated 50–100 million cases occur each year worldwide.

Risk

This disease is rapidly expanding in most tropical and subtropical areas.

Symptoms and sequelae

Symptoms most commonly appear 4–7 days after being bitten by an Aedes aegypti mosquito.

  • High fever
  • Severe frontal headache
  • Joint and muscle pain
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Rash
  • Retro-orbital pain (pain behind the eyes)

Dengue can also present as the severe, sometimes fatal, dengue hemorrhagic fever.

Treatment

  • Take acetaminophen (not aspirin) products to manage fever.
  • Drink plenty of fluids.
  • Rest.
  • Seek prompt infusion of intravenous fluids (in severe cases).
  • DO NOT take acetylsalicylic acid (aspirin) or nonsteroidal anti-inflamma-tory agents (such as ibuprofen), because of their anticoagulant properties, which could potentially worsen dengue hemorrhagic fever.
