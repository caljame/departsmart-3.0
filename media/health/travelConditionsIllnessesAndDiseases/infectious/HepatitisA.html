Hepatitis A

Hepatitis A is an infection of the liver caused by the hepatitis A virus. The virus is spread when a person ingests the hepatitis A virus due to contact with infected persons or contaminated objects, foods, or drinks.

Infection can be spread by:

  • Eating fruits, vegetables, or other contaminated food.
  • Eating raw shellfish harvested from sewage- contaminated water.
  • Swallowing contaminated water or ice.

Risk

Risk is highest for those who live in or travel to countries where Hepatitis A is common.

Prevention

  • Vaccination is recommended to anyone traveling to areas where hepatitis A is common.
  • Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water, especially after using the restroom, changing a diaper, or before preparing or eating food.

Symptoms

Although uncommon, some people have died from hepatitis A. Symptoms of the infection can include one or more of the following:

  • Fever.
  • Malaise.
  • Poor appetite.
  • Abdominal discomfort.
  • Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes) after a few days.
  • Dark urine.
