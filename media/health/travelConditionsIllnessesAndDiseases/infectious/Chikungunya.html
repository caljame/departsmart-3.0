Chikungunya

Chikungunya is a mosquito-borne infection and a major public health problem in many areas of the world, primarily Africa, Asia, and India, and especially the Caribbean. There have been cases reported in Europe and the U.S.

Risk

Ask your travel medicine provider about your destination’s specific risk.

Prevention

  • Mosquito repellants and clothing that reduces the exposure to mosquitoes is strongly recommended.
  • People who sleep during the day are at higher risk and should use bed nets whenever possible.
  • Follow Insect Protection Practices on page 24.

Symptoms and sequelae

Symptoms may develop from 3–7 days after being bitten by an infected mosquito.

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Malaise
  • Joint and muscle pain

Chikungunya infection shares some symptoms with dengue, and the two can be confused. Co-infection is also possible.

Treatment

  • No definitive treatment exists.
  • Supportive and symptomatic treatments are recommended.
  • Aspirin should be avoided.
