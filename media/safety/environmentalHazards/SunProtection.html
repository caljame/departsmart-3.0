Protecting Yourself from the Sun

Your destination may be a hot, humid, or desert- like climate which may be very different from where you live. Protect yourself from the sun’s heat and harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays.

Stay indoors or out of the sun during midday, if possible.

  • Wear clothing that covers your arms and legs.
  • Wear sunglasses that block both UVA and UVB rays.
  • Use sunscreen with SPF 15 or higher and both UVA and UVB protection.
  • Use a minimum SPF 15 lipscreen.
  • Reapply sunscreen and lipscreen per package directions.
  • Keep well hydrated. Drink 8 oz of water, rehydration solution, or diluted sports drinks for each 15 minutes of moderate activity.
  • Avoid alcohol and excess caffeine when at risk for heat illness.
  • Work or take breaks as needed in shaded, cooler areas. Pace yourself; don’t over do it, especially in heat or sun.
